Sheikh et al.'s OM($m$)-based consensus framework for EV energy trading.

consensus models:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{OM($m$)} --- Paper's baseline (Lamport et al., 1982)
\item \textbf{Tower BFT} --- Our proposed extension
\end{enumerate}

The primary focus is on a direct performance and security comparison between \textbf{OM($m$) and Tower BFT}.

\section{Conclusion}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Using the \emph{exact same} IEEE 33-bus EV energy trading parameters from
Sheikh et al.\ ($n{=}51$, $f{\leq}16$, $n_{sen}{=}3854$), Tower BFT achieves:
\begin{itemize}
\item Latency bounds of $100.2$\,ms to $242.9$\,ms dynamically adapting to Byzantine attacks.
\item Near-zero vulnerability under the Static-Temporal Security Framework ($P_{secure} \approx 1.0$).
\item Complete immunity to the recursive latency explosions that paralyze OM($m$).
\end{itemize}

All performance and security metrics have been derived purely through closed-form probabilistic models, eliminating simulation variance and directly mapping algorithm properties to the paper's parameters.
